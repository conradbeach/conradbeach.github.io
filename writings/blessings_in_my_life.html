Blessings in My Life

In 1789, in the first year of his presidency, George Washington issued a proclamation calling for a day of public thanksgiving and prayer.

In part, it said, "Whereas it is the duty of all Nations to acknowledge the providence of Almighty God, to obey his will, to be grateful for his benefits, and humbly to implore his protection and favor…

"Now therefore I do recommend and assign Thursday the 26th day of November next to be devoted by the People of these States to the service of that great and glorious Being, who is the beneficent Author of all the good that was, that is, or that will be—That we may then all unite in rendering unto him our sincere and humble thanks… for the signal and manifold mercies, and the favorable interpositions of his Providence…for the civil and religious liberty with which we are blessed; and the means we have of acquiring and diffusing useful knowledge; and in general for all the great and various favors which he hath been pleased to confer upon us.

"And also that we may then unite in most humbly offering our prayers and supplications to the great Lord and Ruler of Nations and beseech him to pardon our national and other transgressions… and generally to grant unto all Mankind such a degree of temporal prosperity as he alone knows to be best." 1

Abraham Lincoln, in 1863 in the midst of the Civil War, also issued a thanksgiving day proclamation.

He said, "The year that is drawing toward its close has been filled with the blessings of fruitful fields and healthful skies. To these bounties, which are so constantly enjoyed that we are prone to forget the source from which they come, others have been added, which are of so extraordinary a nature that they cannot fail to penetrate and even soften the heart which is habitually insensible to the ever-watchful providence of Almighty God."

He then goes on to describe the many blessings the nation has enjoyed despite the trials of the Civil War.

"No human counsel hath devised, nor hath any mortal hand worked out these great things. They are the gracious gifts of the Most High God, who while dealing with us in anger for our sins, hath nevertheless remembered mercy.

"It has seemed to me fit and proper that they should be solemnly, reverently, and gratefully acknowledged as with one heart and one voice by the whole American people. I do, therefore, invite my fellow-citizens in every part of the United States… to set apart and observe the last Thursday of November next as a Day of Thanksgiving and Praise to our beneficent Father who dwelleth in the heavens. And I recommend to them that, while offering up the ascriptions justly due to Him for such singular deliverances and blessings, they do also, with humble penitence for our national perverseness and disobedience… fervently implore the interposition of the Almighty hand to heal the wounds of the nation, and to restore it, as soon as may be consistent with the Divine purposes, to the full enjoyment of peace, harmony, tranquility, and union." 2

I think these statements are beautiful. I love the principles and attitudes that both of these great men express. They teach the importance of thanking God. They both describe His mercy during difficult times. They both mention the need to repent.

Both Abraham Lincoln and George Washington led this nation through very challenging times.

I don't think our current situation rivals the difficulties of the American Revolution or the Civil War, but we certainly have challenges. A pandemic, a society that seems to be increasingly divided and angry, a decline in moral and religious principles.

No matter the severity of our challenges, the principle of gratitude as these proclamations espoused can help guide us through difficult times.

Washington and Lincoln show us that our gratitude should be directed towards God. Being generally grateful is a good thing, but even better is being grateful towards God.

President Nelson, in his message to the world last week, invited us to do this. He invited us to identify the blessings we have in our life and express gratitude to God for them. And he promised powerful blessings would come as we did that. He promised healing would come. 3

We receive strength, protection and peace as we turn to our Father in Heaven. As we sincerely thank Him for the many blessings and mercies He extends to us, and as we repent and seek His pardon for our sins.

Blessings in My Life

I am incredibly grateful for the many blessings God has given me in my life. While there are many things I'm grateful for, the things I am most grateful for come from the gospel and from the Church. Being a member of the Church of Jesus Christ and being a participant in His gospel has impacted me and helped me in countless ways.

As I contemplated the blessings I have personally received from my Father in Heaven, I identified three areas that mean the most to me.

  1. Guidance
  2. Focus
  3. Joy and Peace

Guidance

President Boyd K. Packer said, "If all you know is what you see with your natural eyes and hear with your natural ears, then you will not know very much". 4

Our vision as mortals is very limited. We just don't know that much. Our minds and spirits are incredible, but they are so limited in comparison to the knowledge and power of God. I'm very grateful that I have access to the knowledge and wisdom of the creator of the universe. It consistently helps me know and understand things I could never learn on my own.

Additionally, there are many sophisticated, false philosophies in this world that Satan uses to distract and mislead people. The guidance of my Father in Heaven protects me from these false philosophies and beliefs.

Doctrine and Covenants 82 gives a definition of commandments that I really like. It says, "And again, I say unto you, I give unto you a new commandment, that you may understand my will concerning you; Or, in other words, I give unto you directions how you may act before me, that it may turn to you for your salvation." 5

God's work and glory is to bring to pass our immortality and eternal life. That's his purpose. 6 Everything he does, everything he asks us to do, is in pursuit of that goal. I'm grateful for the commandments Heavenly Father gives to us because they show me how to live a good life and avoid pain, difficulty and sorrow.

I have confidence that I can and will receive guidance and direction from my Father in Heaven as I live righteously and seek His guidance. He helps me see what I should be doing in my life, and He gives me a much greater ability to discern truth.

Personally, I am especially grateful for the gift of living prophets. I am so thankful for the leaders of this church, for the counsel, guidance and wisdom they give to us. To be led by prophets, seers and revelators that guide this church under the direction of Jesus Christ is an immense blessing.

Following the words of the prophets has helped me avoid many mistakes in my life and find a great deal more success than I otherwise would have.

I have come to really appreciate the gift of the Holy Ghost and the power it gives us. I learned this especially over the course of my mission. It's something my mission president taught us about often. Elder Lawrence E. Corbridge of the Seventy said, "Nothing in this life is of greater worth than the supernal gift of the Holy Ghost." 7 I have a testimony of that statement.

The Holy Ghost teaches us, sanctifies us and directs us.

One thing I really appreciate about the Church is it puts me in a situation where I am constantly encouraged to be better and do better. And the Gift of the Holy Ghost and the atonement of Jesus Christ give me added power to do that.

The sanctification that comes through the gift of the Holy Ghost allows me to change in ways that I could never do on my own.

Focus

In section 25 of the Doctrine and Covenants, the Lord instructs Emma Smith by saying, "And verily I say unto thee that thou shalt lay aside the things of this world, and seek for the things of a better." 8 Certainly, that's God's invitation to all of us.

The gospel and church of Christ bring simplicity and focus into my life. The gospel helps me focus on the things that actually matter and ignore or minimize the many, many things that don't.

It helps me focus on the most important things such as family, helping others and becoming more like Jesus Christ. The gospel gives me a constant reminder that things like money, physical possessions and worldly success just don't matter eternally.

The gospel helps me focus on building God's kingdom instead of my own. It helps me see just how much better my life is when I spend my time participating in God's mission instead of selfishly pursuing my own interests.

I recently read a statement from President M. Russell Ballard that I really like. He said "Simplicity is powerful. Complexity is dangerous. I think Lucifer is the master of complexity and the Lord is the master of simplicity. The Gospel is simple and it is simply beautiful." 9

I am very grateful for that simplicity in my life. Being a follower of Jesus Christ removes a lot of complexity. The more closely I follow Him, the more I feel various burdens and concerns disappear.

Joy and Peace

Finally, I am grateful for the joy and peace that I have because of the church and gospel of Christ. I remember multiples times on my mission people asking me things like, "Elder Beach, why are you so happy?" "Elder Beach, why are you always smiling?" I was happy because of the things I knew from God about His plan and because I was doing what He wanted me to do by sharing that knowledge with others. I continue to receive joy as I choose to follow Him.

The Book of Mormon teaches that "Men are that they might have joy". 10 God's plan is the instruction manual for finding that joy.

Because I'm a member of Christ's church and because I have accepted His gospel, I have a power to overcome difficulty and find joy in my life that I could never have on my own.
