Legacy of Encouragement

Back in 2005, the well known historian David McCullough gave an address at BYU where he discussed different events from the American Revolution in the year 1776. He described the many defeats that the Continental Army experienced during that year, the harsh conditions of Valley Forge and the miraculous, successful battles at Trenton and Princeton near the end of 1776. By that point in time, George Washington's army had been reduced to only a few thousand men. And while the effect of their recent victories was substantial, the army and the cause of the Revolution weren't exactly in a great place. After describing these events, David McCullough told the following story.

He said, "I want to share a scene that took place on the last day of the year of 1776, Dec. 31. All the enlistments for the entire army were up. Every soldier, because of the system at the time, was free to go home as of the first day of January 1777. Washington called a large part of the troops out into formation. He appeared in front of these ragged men on his horse, and he urged them to reenlist. He said that if they would sign up for another six months, he'd give them a bonus of 10 dollars. It was an enormous amount then because that's about what they were being paid for a month—if and when they could get paid. These were men who were desperate for pay of any kind. Their families were starving.

"The drums rolled, and he asked those who would stay on to step forward. The drums kept rolling, and nobody stepped forward. Washington turned and rode away from them. Then he stopped, and he turned back and rode up to them again. This is what we know he said:

" 'My brave fellows, you have done all I asked you to do, and more than could be reasonably expected, but your country is at stake, your wives, your houses, and all that you hold dear. You have worn yourselves out with fatigues and hardships, but we know not how to spare you. If you will consent to stay one month longer, you will render that service to the cause of liberty, and to your country, which you can probably never do under any other circumstance.'

"Again the drums rolled. This time the men began stepping forward. 'God Almighty,' wrote Nathanael Greene, 'inclined their hearts to listen to the proposal and they engaged anew.' " 1

I love that story. There's a lot of beautiful stories like that from the American Revolution. A lot of miracles.

In the Doctrine and Covenants, we're taught that the Revolution was inspired and supported by God. 2 He redeemed this promised land and established a free nation in preparation for the restoration of His gospel and His church. The struggle those men in this story were engaged in truly was the work of God. And their sacrifice has blessed countless people throughout the world. But at the time, how much of that could they have seen or known. And even if they could see it, how hard was that to remember while freezing and starving in Valley Forge or fighting a seemingly hopeless war against the most powerful military in the world. Without the encouragement of an inspired George Washington, it seems likely they would have abandoned the cause. Even with their recent successes, they didn't want to go on. But go on they did. Something we should be very grateful for.

At least in some ways, I think our situation is not all that different from those men standing in front of Washington on a cold December day in 1776. We're also part of an eternally significant cause. Gathering Israel. Spreading the gospel. Preparing the world for the Second Coming of Jesus Christ. President Nelson said, "[The gathering of Israel] is the most important thing taking place on earth today." 3 But despite the importance and significance of what we're a part of, we may at times not want to continue. At times it will be challenging (we've been warned that the days ahead may make it very challenging). At other times we may just get distracted and lose focus of what's most important. But similar to what George Washington said, we have the opportunity to serve the work of God in a way we can probably never do under any other circumstance.

And just like those soldiers in Washington's army, there may be times when the only thing that keeps us going is the support and encouragement of others. There may be times when the only thing that keeps someone else going is your support, encouragement and example.

And of course, there are many who simply don't know the things we know who need to hear our testimonies and invitations so they have an opportunity to join this work as well.

None of us can make it to where God wants us to go without His divine aid. We won't come through to the other side without the help of our Father in Heaven, without the guidance of the Holy Ghost, and without the redeeming and enabling power of the atonement of Jesus Christ.

Jesus Christ said, "In the world ye shall have tribulation: but be of good cheer; I have overcome the world." 4

He said, "I am the vine, ye are the branches: He that abideth in me, and I in him, the same bringeth forth much fruit: for without me ye can do nothing." 5

It is through the power of Jesus Christ that we succeed in this life. Jesus Christ is the light and the life and the truth of the world. 6 He is the light we hold up. His is the truth that we champion.

It's essential that we hold fast to our Savior and do the things He's asked us to do. And it's essential that He be the source of power that we point others to.

The world certainly doesn't make any of that easy. Lehi's vision of the tree of life seems more and more real to me. The great and spacious building with it's finery and mocking voices; the mists of darkness constantly working to blind and deceive. The world is full of deception, distraction and temptation. But when we build our foundation firmly on the Lord Jesus Christ and His gospel, when we hold fast to the iron rod, we can be confident that the power of God will guide and deliver us.

It's critical to remember that. And it's critical that we share that with others and help them remember it. In a world that's full of darkness and confusion, we can do so much to help each other find and hold on to the truth.

And with so many opposing voices and philosophies, it's never been more important for us to hold up the truth of the gospel and the light of Jesus Christ.

Elder Neal A. Maxwell of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles said, "Let us be articulate, for while our defense of the kingdom may not stir all hearers, the absence of thoughtful response may cause fledglings among the faithful to falter. What we assert may not be accepted, but unasserted convictions soon become deserted convictions." 7

And Brother Douglas D. Holmes of the Young Men General Presidency said, "[Y]ou must recognize that many of the popular, reasonable, and appealing philosophies of our day… simply don't withstand examination under the pure light of truth. Seeking, living by, and defending truth are absolutely critical to your spiritual and temporal survival. We cannot simply adopt, even unwittingly, the philosophies of men absent the light of revelation. I invite you to continue to be diligent seekers and defenders of truth." 8

One of the things that discourages me sometimes is how increasingly secular our society seems to be becoming. There are times and places where secular philosophies and scientific theories are fully accepted and discussed but religious thinking or principles are sidelined or outright disallowed. There's nothing inherently wrong, of course, with secular philosophies or scientific theories; there can be truth there too. But spiritual and religious truth ought to be valued and included in the conversation as well. And, in reality, given greater weight than those other worldly sources of knowledge.

I'm personally very grateful for individuals who push back against this trend of secularism by openly expressing their faith. Sometimes, when I'm in these more secular environments, it can seem like the believers are the minority and that there's very few people left who have faith in God. That's simply not the case though. So it's very encouraging and reassuring to me when I see things like a coworker expressing their faith in God or offering to pray for a colleague. Or when someone in an otherwise non-religious article or video shares their simple testimony. I believe that's what Christ expects from us. He wants disciples who aren't afraid to show their faith and belief even in a society that is in many ways moving away from such things. He wants disciples who consistently say, "I believe." Disciples who consistently prioritize the things of God over the things of the world. And who trust the teachings of God over the teachings of the world. Despite the scoffing from the great and spacious building; despite the theories and philosophies of men that question or attack gospel truths, we the followers of Christ will courageously say, "We believe." I believe our willingness to do that will shine light into the hearts and minds of others. And it will lift and encourage fellow believers.

Last month (Janauary 2023) in a Young Adult Face to Face, Elder Jeffrey R. Holland shared this counsel, "[W]e call you and every other young Latter-day Saint to be in the forefront of the moral force that can resolve these problems, that can turn back the tide of fear and pessimism and anxiety surrounding us. How important it is for you to pray not only for the Lord to prevail in your lives, as President Russell M. Nelson has asked, but also to pray that the values of your life will prevail with others who aren't quite so sure yet. If as individual disciples of the Lord Jesus Christ we were all more loving and peaceful and kind, if we would all try to keep the commandments of God as best we can, then we have every reason to feel confident about the world's condition and our own. Walking into the future this way, filled with peace and godly promise, we could have an absolutely stunning impact on the world. Abraham Lincoln said once that he tried to pluck a weed and plant a flower in its place every chance he got. If we all did that, our moral and spiritual deserts would be veritable gardens in no time… Refuse to accept the world for what it appears to be. Shine the brightness of your hope on it, and make it what it ought to be. Be that light Sister Holland has asked for you to be, a light never to be extinguished, the light of the Savior of the world." 9

We're not so powerless to impact this world as it may sometimes seem, at least as it sometimes seems to me. Despite the intensifying darkness, light, as it always does, will drive the darkness out. And despite the challenges we face, when we think of all that God and Jesus Christ have done for us and the many miracles They will do for us in the future 10, we can be filled with hope. And eventually, hopefully soon, Jesus Christ will return and make all things right.

I'll finish with another quote from Elder Neal A. Maxwell. He said, "How can we remain silent when we know that there will come a time, as the scriptures have foretold, when there will be a great sign in heaven and that all flesh shall see Christ together (See D&C 88:93, D&C 101:23)? No wonder we rejoice! No wonder we reach out! No wonder we stand all amazed whenever the light of the everlasting gospel lights up a soul, denoting the dawn of a new discipleship... As the veil of unbelief thickens around the globe, nothing can rend 'the dark veil of unbelief' (Alma 19:6) that is not sharp, piercing, bright, and true. Dull disciples will not light the way nor draw people to the kingdom. The philosophies of the world cannot do it... The truths we seek to live and to share are sweet, reassuring, and redeeming. But they are also tough truths; they keep us up against things that really matter. And central to all of these truths is the declaration of the Savior himself in which he said, 'Behold, . . . I am the light, and the life, and the truth of the world' (Ether 4:12)." 7

I am eternally grateful for the blessing of being a member of the restored church and gospel of Jesus Christ. I am grateful for the knowledge I have. It gives me direction, assurance and foundation. I know that Jesus Christ is the savior and redeemer of the world. I know this is His church. And I know He is coming back again. I pray that each of us may constantly strengthen our faith in these truths and find opportunities to help others do the same. I'm grateful that we get live on the earth right now; it's an amazing time to be here. Through the power of our Lord Jesus Christ, we will see and accomplish incredible things.

Originally written in January 2023 for a sacrament meeting talk in the Wellsville 8th Ward in Wellsville, Utah.
